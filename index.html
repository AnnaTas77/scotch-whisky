“Scotch Whisky” is defined in The Scotch Whisky Regulations 2009. In summary, it is:

  • Made in Scotland from only cereals, water and yeast;
  • Matured for a minimum of three years in oak casks; and
  • Bottled at a minimum strength of 40% alcohol by volume (ABV).

Categories of Scotch Whisky

There are five categories of Scotch Whisky.

Single Malt Scotch Whisky

“Single Malt Scotch Whisky” means a Scotch Whisky that has been distilled in one or more batches at a single distillery, from water and malted barley without the addition of any other cereals, and in pot stills.

Single Grain Scotch Whisky

“Single Grain Scotch Whisky” means a Scotch Whisky that has been distilled at a single distillery except Single Malt Scotch Whisky or a Blended Scotch Whisky.

Blended Malt Scotch Whisky

“Blended Malt Scotch Whisky” means a blend of two or more Single Malt Scotch Whiskies that have been distilled at more than one distillery.

Blended Grain Scotch Whisky

“Blended Grain Scotch Whisky” means a blend of two or more Single Grain Scotch Whiskies that have been distilled at more than one distillery.

Blended Scotch Whisky

“Blended Scotch Whisky” means a blend of one or more Single Malt Scotch Whiskies with one or more Single Grain Scotch Whiskies.

Regions of Scotch Whisky

There are five regions that produce Scotch Whisky. Each region’s whiskies are influenced by factors such as local water sources, climate, and traditional production methods.

Highlands

The largest whisky-producing region, known for a wide range of styles from light and floral to rich and smoky.

Glenmorangie 10 Year Old

Fruity and smooth with hints of citrus and vanilla.

Dalmore 12 Year Old

Rich and full-bodied with notes of chocolate and spices.

Oban 14 Year Old

Balanced with a hint of smoke and a maritime influence.

Lowlands

Produces lighter, more delicate whiskies with a gentle, floral character.

Auchentoshan 12 Year Old

A triple-distilled whisky with a light and floral character, often noted for its sweetness and hints of toffee.

Glenkinchie 12 Year Old

Light and floral with a touch of sweetness and spice.

Bladnoch 10 Year Old

Fresh and citrusy with a creamy texture and a hint of malt.

Speyside

Home to the highest concentration of distilleries, known for producing whiskies with a sweet, fruity, and elegant profile.

Glenfiddich 12 Year Old

Fruity and floral with notes of pear and apple.

Macallan 12 Year Old

Rich and complex with sherry influences and dried fruits.

Balvenie DoubleWood 12 Year Old

Sweet and honeyed with layers of vanilla and oak.

Islay

Famous for its peaty, smoky whiskies with strong maritime influences, often described as robust and medicinal.

Laphroaig 10 Year Old

Peaty and medicinal with intense smoke and seaweed notes.

Ardbeg 10 Year Old

Smoky and peaty with hints of citrus and a touch of sweetness.

Lagavulin 16 Year Old

Rich and smoky with a maritime character and notes of peat.

Campbeltown

Once a thriving whisky region, now with only a few distilleries, known for producing whiskies with a distinctive salty, briny character.

Springbank 10 Year Old

Complex with a balance of peat, fruit, and maritime influences.

Glen Scotia Double Cask

Fruity and spicy with a touch of brine and smoke.

Kilkerran 12 Year Old

Well-balanced with flavors of fruit, malt, and a hint of peat.
